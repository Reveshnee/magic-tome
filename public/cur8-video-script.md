# Cur8 Tutorial Video Script

**Duration: ~5-7 minutes**

---

## [INTRO — 0:00-0:15]

"Hi! I'm going to show you Cur8 — a personal haven for saving, organizing, and discovering meaningful content. Whether you're a researcher, creator, student, or just someone who saves things you want to revisit, Cur8 helps you build a living library that actually makes sense.

Let me walk you through everything."

---

## [SIGN IN — 0:15-0:30]

**On screen: Show the login page**

"When you open Cur8, you'll see a login screen. Sign in with your email and password. Once you're in, you'll land on your home screen — your haven.

And here's the magic: everything you see is personalized to you."

---

## [HOME SCREEN OVERVIEW — 0:30-1:30]

**On screen: Pan around the home screen slowly**

"At the top is a beautiful hero image — it changes with the seasons. Below that, you have several sections, each a starting point for different ways to use Cur8.

**First: 'Know Yourself'** — This is your AI companion. It lets you explore topics, ask questions about your saved content, and keeps track of all your past conversations. More on that in a moment.

**Next: 'The Pond'** — Your daily rituals. Four interactive orbs: Intention (to set your focus for the day), Breathwork (guided breathing for calm), Focus (ambient sounds + timer for deep work), and Calendar (to track your activities).

**Then: 'Recently Visited'** — The last 8 things you opened. Quick way to get back to what you're working on.

**Your Havens** — These are your 8 curated spaces. The Koi Pond for learning, The Current for work, Sanctuary for wellness, The Grove for psychology, Ember for inspiration, Bloom for entertainment, The Greenhouse for style, and The Tide for documents. Each one is a different flavour of your life.

At the bottom, you can see a word map — tap any word to filter your content across all havens."

---

## [SAVING CONTENT — 1:30-2:45]

**On screen: Show the floating "+" button**

"Now, how do you actually save things? Easy. There's a gold '+' button floating at the bottom of the screen. Tap it.

**On screen: Show the save dialog**

You can paste a link from YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, articles — anything with a URL. You can also upload files: PDFs, Word docs, images, voice notes, anything.

**On screen: Paste a link, show it loading**

Once you paste, the app fetches the title, description, and thumbnail automatically. Then you choose which haven to save it to.

**On screen: Select a haven**

You can add your own notes, attach files, or just save it bare. Hit save.

**On screen: Show the save confirmation**

And it's in. One tap, one URL, and you've captured something worth remembering."

---

## [BRAIN DUMP & REFLECTIONS — 2:45-3:45]

**On screen: Show the Brain Dump button**

"But what about ideas that don't have a URL? That's where Brain Dump comes in. It's for fleeting thoughts.

**On screen: Tap Brain Dump**

Type your thought or tap the mic to dictate. You can even use AI to 'Tidy' your thought — it'll automatically pull out action items and key points.

**On screen: Show the Tidy feature working**

When you save, your thought can be sent straight to your email or any app you choose — so your ideas never get lost in the ether.

For deeper self-reflection, there's also Reflections. Same idea, but more structured. Tap 'Reflect', choose or write a question, record your answer, and it's saved as a thread you can revisit whenever you want to see how your thinking has evolved."

---

## [OPENING A HAVEN — 3:45-4:45]

**On screen: Click on a haven (e.g., The Koi Pond)**

"Let's open one of the havens. I'll click The Koi Pond.

**On screen: Show the haven page with three panels — videos left, preview centre, docs right**

Here's what you see: On the left, all your saved items. In the middle, a preview of what you've selected. On the right, documents and linked websites.

**On screen: Tap an item**

When I tap an item, it opens in the preview. If it's a video, it plays. If it's a document, I can read it right here. The app has actually read and understood the full document, so I can ask the AI about it.

**On screen: Show the 'Ask AI' button**

See this button? 'Ask AI'. I can ask questions about this specific document and the AI will answer based on what it's read. No guessing, no 'I don't have access' — it has the full text.

**On screen: Show the sort and filter buttons**

Above your items, you can sort by newest, oldest, alphabetically, recently opened. You can filter by type — just videos, or just documents. You can create folders to organize things, and you can select multiple items to move, copy, or delete in bulk."

---

## [THE POND RITUALS — 4:45-5:30]

**On screen: Go back to home, show The Pond**

"Let me show you The Pond rituals quickly.

**On screen: Tap Intention orb**

Intention helps you set your focus for the day. You can write, record, or ask the AI for guidance on what your intention should be.

**On screen: Exit, tap Breathwork**

Breathwork gives you guided breathing exercises — box breath, 4-7-8 breathing, whatever your nervous system needs.

**On screen: Exit, tap Focus**

Focus gives you ambient sounds — rain, ocean, forest, binaural beats — plus a timer for deep work. So you've got sound, you've got time tracking, and you can mark down how focused you felt when you're done.

**On screen: Exit, tap Calendar**

Calendar shows you what you've been up to. Which dates you saved things, which days you worked on your rituals. It's a visual timeline of your practice."

---

## [KNOW YOURSELF (AI HUB) — 5:30-6:15]

**On screen: Go back to home, tap Know Yourself section**

"One more powerful feature: Know Yourself. This is the AI Hub.

You can chat with the AI about any topic — productivity, wellness, creativity — and the AI has read all your saved content. So when you ask questions, it's answering in the context of everything you've collected.

**On screen: Show past discussions list**

All your conversations are saved. You can revisit them, continue them, share them, or export them. It's like having a personal advisor who knows everything you've saved."

---

## [MOBILE & CALM MODE — 6:15-6:45]

**On screen: Switch to mobile view or show on phone**

"Cur8 works on phone, tablet, and desktop. On mobile, the layout adapts so you can type easily in Brain Dump, read documents comfortably, and browse your havens with one hand.

**On screen: Tap Calm button**

You can also enable Calm Mode. It pauses all animations, stops notifications, and makes the interface peaceful. Perfect for when you just want to browse your content without distraction."

---

## [CLOSING — 6:45-7:00]

"That's Cur8. Save what matters. Organize it your way. Discover it again when you need it. Your haven is waiting.

Questions? There's an in-app guide and you can always reach out. Thanks for watching."

## Filming Tips:

1. **Use Calm Mode for cleaner screenshots** — Turn off animations so viewers focus on the content, not the motion
2. **Speak slowly and pause between sections** — Let viewers absorb what they're seeing
3. **Zoom in on buttons** — Make it clear where to tap
4. **Show real examples** — Use actual content you've saved rather than demo content
5. **Pause on each screen for 2-3 seconds** — Gives viewers time to follow along
6. **Use clear B-roll** — Show the same feature from multiple angles if helpful
